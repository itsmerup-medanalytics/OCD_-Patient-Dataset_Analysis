# OCD-Patient-Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes a dataset of patients with Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD) to uncover patterns, trends, and correlations among demographics, clinical profiles, symptom severity, and treatment responses. The goal is to provide actionable insights to support research and clinical decision-making.

## Analysis Performed
- Explored patient demographics including age, gender, ethnicity, education, and marital status.  
- Analyzed OCD clinical profiles, including obsession and compulsion types.  
- Assessed severity of symptoms using obsession and compulsion scores.  
- Investigated comorbid conditions such as anxiety and depression.  
- Handled missing data by retaining unavailable clinical information.  
- Created visualizations to summarize distributions, correlations, and patterns.

## Tools and Technologies Used
- **Python** – Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn  
- **Jupyter Notebook** – Step-by-step analysis and visualization

## Key Insights & Observations
- **Age:** Higher OCD prevalence is observed in adult and older age groups.  
- **Gender:** Male and female patients show nearly equal distribution.  
- **Ethnicity:** OCD occurs across multiple ethnic groups; Caucasian patients show higher numbers.  
- **Education Level:** Patients from varied educational backgrounds are affected.  
- **Marital Status:** OCD occurs across all marital categories.  
- **Family History:** A significant subset of patients has a family history of OCD.  
- **Clinical Profile:** Obsession and compulsion types vary widely.  
- **Severity:** Obsession and compulsion scores show comparable severity.  
- **Comorbidities:** Anxiety and depression commonly coexist with OCD.  
- **Data Handling:** Missing clinical information was retained as unavailable.

## Conclusions
- OCD affects patients across age, gender, ethnicity, and education levels.  
- Family history and comorbidities play a key role in the clinical profile.  
- Severity of obsession and compulsion is comparable across patients.  
- Data analysis and visualization provide actionable insights for clinicians and researchers to understand patterns and trends in OCD patient population.
